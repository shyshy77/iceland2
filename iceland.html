THE LAND OF FIRE AND ICE

Experiencing this otherworldly landscape was like a cinematic movie where at times, what I was seeing was not even real. Especially when a solar flare decides to erupt and light up the sky with dancing green lights. Magical is the best way I can explain this small country located in the North-Atlantic ocean close to the arctic circle. Only 5 hours and 45minutes flight from NYC, tourist-friendly and also cost-effective, making it so easy to travel. Iceland is a different kind of vacation. There is so much to do and see, and different seasons create it’s own kind of adventure. I’ve visited in the months of March, April and November, hoping to catch the peak of the northern lights. They showed up in two out my three visits. One especially spectacular night. The glaciers in winter gives the landscape another layer of snow. Hiking and snowmobiling this terrain makes for a thrilling adventure.
